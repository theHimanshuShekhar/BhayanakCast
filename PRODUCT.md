# Product

## Register

product

## Users

BhayanakCast serves Discord-authenticated users who want small, public or private watch-together rooms around live screen/application streams. They are usually browsing active rooms, joining a room with up to 10 members, choosing which member streams to watch, chatting, reviewing public profiles, or handling platform moderation as a trusted admin.

## Product Purpose

BhayanakCast is a public discovery platform for small social streaming rooms. It makes live rooms easy to find, safe to join, and explicit to watch: members choose stream subscriptions, hosts own room-level moderation, and platform admins handle reports, sanctions, live-room intervention, and aggregate-backed operational views. Success means users can understand room state quickly, join without ambiguity, control their own stream/watch actions, and trust privacy/moderation boundaries.

## Brand Personality

Dense, social, energetic. The interface should feel like a live operations deck for watch-together communities: compact, immediate, high-signal, and slightly electric without becoming noisy or gamer-cluttered. Copy should be direct and product-specific, using BhayanakCast language such as Room Member, Host, Stream, Stream Preview, Past Stream, Report, and Platform Admin accurately.

## Anti-references

Avoid generic SaaS dashboards, cream marketing pages, gradient-text hero treatments, overdecorated ghost cards, decorative glassmorphism, and generic “broadcast” language. Avoid layouts that imply a single streamer/audience model, hide private-room constraints, or make moderation feel secondary. Avoid low-contrast gray text on dark/tinted panels, controls that only reveal on hover, and motion that distracts from room state.

## Design Principles

1. Make live state legible before decoration: room capacity, stream count, privacy, host, and watch/subscription state must be readable at a glance.
2. Preserve explicit control: joining, watching, streaming, leaving, reporting, banning, and sanctioning should look intentional and reversible where the product allows it.
3. Keep density disciplined: compact panels and mono rhythm are part of the product, but hierarchy, spacing, and focus states must prevent cognitive blur.
4. Safety is a first-class workflow: private-room admission, report targets, room bans, platform sanctions, and admin intervention should be visible, trustworthy, and never euphemized.
5. Match the product model, not the prototype when they conflict: rooms contain multiple member streams; Past Streams are metadata records, not replay recordings.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG 2.2 AA. Dialogs, menus, tabs, stream controls, moderation controls, and auth actions must be keyboard operable with visible focus states and meaningful labels. Body and placeholder text must meet contrast requirements against their actual surfaces. Reduced motion must disable non-essential animation while preserving state feedback. Do not rely on color alone for live/private/error/moderation state.
