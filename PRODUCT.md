# BhayanakCast V1 Product Requirements

## Product promise

BhayanakCast is a public community clubhouse for small social screen-sharing rooms. People discover active rooms, join with a Discord account, explicitly choose streams to watch, chat, and move between rooms. The rewrite preserves the documented V1 functional contract while rebuilding the implementation and product UI.

## Audience and access

- The product is a general-audience, English-only community.
- Any Discord-authenticated Account may create or join rooms.
- Discord sign-in is sufficient for participation; relevant terms appear only in the account-deletion confirmation, while community rules remain discoverable and enforceable without an acknowledgement gate.
- Anonymous visitors may browse discovery and full public profiles, but cannot create or join rooms, use room chat/media, moderate, or request account deletion.
- Discord sign-in initiated from a room Join action returns to that room's current pre-admission view without auto-joining. Only the room identifier survives OAuth; private passwords never do and must be re-entered.
- Discord identity mirrors the current Discord display name/avatar on every sign-in. There is no local username or profile edit.
- Discord Accounts without an email remain eligible. Authentication stores a non-routable internal placeholder derived from the stable Discord provider ID; it is never displayed, contacted, searched, or treated as verified.
- Signed-in sessions use a seven-day rolling lifetime and refresh their expiry at most once per day while active. Sign-out, approved account deletion, all-access restriction, or credential invalidation revokes access immediately.

## Discovery and public presence

- Home uses three responsive stages while preserving the center order of search/filters, Live Rooms including the featured room, then ten recent Past Streams. Wide (≥1280px) uses a left identity/action sidebar, central discovery column, and right statistics/action rail. Medium (768–1279px) keeps a compact left sidebar, removes the right rail, and collapses global statistics inside search utilities. Small (<768px) replaces both rails with a top brand bar containing `B`, connected-Account count, and avatar or Log in, plus persistent bottom Home/Create/Profile navigation; fixed bars honor safe areas, visible focus, and touch targets. There is no title/create toolbar or promotional hero.
- The wide left sidebar shows a large `B`, distinct connected signed-in Account count with presence icon, Create Room, and a bottom signed-in avatar popout containing Profile and Log out. The wide right rail shows Live Rooms, active Streams, current Room Memberships, rooms created today, and today's peak connected Accounts, plus either a compact button that opens the shared Create Room dialog or anonymous Discord Log in; daily metrics use the operator timezone.
- Home uses one document scroll. Wide rails and the medium left rail remain sticky/viewport-height without nested scrolling, falling into document flow if they cannot fit. Search utilities are sticky only while any query/category/tag is active and sit below the small top bar; clearing all returns normal flow. Small bottom navigation remains fixed.
- Active Rooms rank by current member count descending, active stream count descending, then most recent meaningful room activity.
- Wide Home opens with the featured rank-1 card spanning two rows on the left and ranks 2–3 stacked on the right; rank 4 onward uses an equal-size two-column grid. Medium uses a full-width feature plus two columns; small uses one ranked column. Missing slots have no placeholders. Counts/state update live, but feature/order remain stable until reload, return navigation, search/filter context change, explicit refresh, or successful canonical refresh after realtime reconnection; ended rooms close cells without promotion, and search results remain uniform.
- Every Live Room card combines room metadata/state/presence with a one-to-four-tile Stream Preview mosaic. More than four active Streams use the four freshest previews; public tiles are unblurred and private tiles blurred. The featured card gives both mosaic and metadata more area, while no-Stream cards use real presence/state/metadata instead of fake media. Public cards show an avatar stack/count but no names; private cards expose no participant identities.
- A Live Room card is one accessible link target; clicking or activating anywhere opens the room's pre-admission page. Descriptive chips are not nested controls. The destination completes authentication/admission and explicit Join before rendering the admitted room layout or creating membership, peer, chat, or media state.
- The home/discovery page shows the ten most recently ended Past Streams below Live Rooms, including when none are live. Desktop uses a compact two-column metadata list and mobile one column; items show name, end time, visibility, optional category/tags, participation/Stream summary, and Open summary action. Past Streams have no image/fake thumbnail, carousel, search, pagination, dedicated browse surface, or replay media.
- When no rooms are live outside search/filter results, replace the featured/list area with a restrained text-led invitation: “The clubhouse is quiet,” concise public/private-room context, and Create Room. Use no illustration or onboarding flow; retain Past Streams below, or show one first-community cue when history is empty. Anonymous activation initiates Discord sign-in and returns Home without auto-creation.
- In-app discovery searches Active Room names/categories/tags and public-profile identity. Wide/medium show searchable Category single-select and Tags multi-select comboboxes; small uses a Filters bottom sheet. Options are normalized distinct values on current Active Rooms with counts; active filters are removable chips with conditional Clear all. Category matches normalized exact value and multiple tags use AND. Text ranks exact/prefix/substring before conservative fuzzy matches for queries of at least three characters. A query replaces normal Home with separate uniform Active Rooms and Public Profiles groups; filters affect rooms only, and clearing restores Home. Search never returns Past Streams as results, chat, reports, media, private transcripts/preferences, sanctions, or live hidden participant identities.
- Home text search uses TanStack Pacer `useDebouncer`, trailing after 250 ms or flushing on Enter; filters apply immediately. Canonical query/category/tag URL parameters form TanStack Query keys and restore share/reload/return state while edits replace the current history entry. Query functions forward their `AbortSignal`; TanStack Query v5 uses `placeholderData: keepPreviousData` and `isPlaceholderData` to preserve prior results while the next key loads, and accessible progress/count announcements do not move focus.
- Each Public Profile result is one accessible link showing Discord avatar/name, compact aggregate usage statistics, three most recent Past Streams, and top three co-users. Excerpts are non-interactive, missing rows collapse, and the grouped search response supplies bounded public data without per-card requests or private/live-hidden content.
- Active Room search results form one compact horizontal list with a small one-to-four Preview mosaic and allowed room metadata/state/presence; small screens stack only when needed for readability. Direct matches precede fuzzy matches, then ties use normal social rank. Public Profile ties use normalized display name then opaque ID, never popularity. Each result remains one link.
- TanStack Query is the only Home domain fetching/cache boundary; Better Auth separately owns session state. Server rendering blocks on and hydrates discovery/search plus visible Past Streams; facets, statistics, and connected presence prefetch into independent skeleton/error boundaries. Stale times are 15s for rooms/presence, 30s for facets/statistics, and 60s for profiles/Past Streams; inactive data is garbage-collected after 10 minutes. Stale queries refetch on mount, focus, network reconnect, URL-key change, or Retry without polling. Socket.IO patches value-only cached fields and targets invalidation when result membership may change. Realtime loss marks values stale; reconnect invalidates/refetches active Home keys and recomputes rank once.
- The home/discovery page and full public profiles are search-indexable.
- Individual room URLs, including ended/Past Stream room views, are noindex.
- Public profiles expose Discord-mirrored identity, aggregate usage statistics, Past Stream history, and top co-users.
- Top co-users rank by concurrent room-membership time, not media or chat activity.
- Active Accounts cannot hide profile fields; account deletion is the sole removal/anonymization path.

## Rooms and membership

- A room is public or private and has a hard cap of 10 Room Members, including members who only chat or watch.
- An Account holds one live room membership at a time. To switch rooms, the server validates target admission before leaving the current room; a failed target admission preserves current membership, while a successful switch stops current media/subscription and triggers prior-room lifecycle effects.
- Ordinary non-streaming, non-Host members leave immediately. Leaving as a Host or active streamer requires a confirmation that explains the resulting Host transfer and/or Stream stop, including during a room switch or new-room creation.
- Full rooms remain in discovery with an explicit Full state; joining is disabled until capacity opens, with no waitlist, reservation, notification, or Host approval queue.
- Create Room defaults to Public; selecting Private requires a shared password of at least eight characters.
- Create Room requires only a trimmed name of 3–80 user-visible characters. Category is optional freeform text up to 32 characters, as are up to five optional freeform tags of up to 24 characters each; V1 has no long-form description or predefined taxonomy.
- Live and historical room names need not be unique; opaque room IDs remain authoritative for links, admission, history, reports, and realtime commands.
- A successful creation immediately enters the creator as Host with no lobby/interstitial. If the creator is currently in another room, creation validates before closing that membership; failure changes neither current membership nor visible rooms.
- Every Home Create affordance opens the same form: centered modal on wide/medium and full-screen safe-area/keyboard-aware dialog on small, with identical fields/defaults/validation, explicit Cancel/Create, focus trap/return, and dismiss behavior. Anonymous activation signs in first and carries only an opaque create intent; Home then reopens a blank dialog. No draft/password crosses OAuth and creation is never automatic.
- Public rooms are openly joinable while capacity and safety gates permit.
- Opening a live-room URL shows public pre-admission metadata/status and never joins automatically. A signed-in Account must explicitly select Join; private admission validates its password, and room chat/media/live participant detail begins only after admission succeeds.
- Private rooms remain listed in discovery with a lock state, hide live participant identities, and require a shared password of at least eight characters.
- A Host may rotate but never retrieve a private room password without evicting existing members. Future/rejoining members must use the new password.
- The current Host may change live room name, category, tags, and visibility. A Public-to-Private save requires a new valid password and retains current members; a Private-to-Public change requires confirmation, clears its password, and retains current members.
- After a private room becomes a Past Stream, its participation may appear in public profiles and top co-user data.
- An unexpected disconnect reserves a member slot for 45 seconds; the same Account automatically reclaims it without repeated admission, but any Stream stops and must be explicitly restarted. Intentional leave, kick, ban, connection displacement, room end, and admission loss have no grace. After expiry, normal host handoff, five-minute empty-room grace, and Past Stream transition apply.
- After the final member leaves, a room stays discoverable and joinable under current public/private gates for five minutes. The first eligible joiner revives it and becomes Host; otherwise it becomes a Past Stream. Revival starts fresh membership intervals and does not restore stopped Streams or prior live-only state.
- A room expires 12 hours after original creation; no Host transfer, revival, reconnect, or update resets or extends it. Persistent countdown state and Activity warnings appear at 30, 10, and 1 minute. Expiry stops media, closes memberships, and creates the normal Past Stream without grace.
- When a Host permanently leaves, the server transfers Host authority to the longest continuously present remaining Room Member; V1 has no election, acceptance, or preference flow.
- A newly accepted WebSocket connection becomes an Account’s sole active connection across the app. The server disconnects all older Account connections without a confirmation flow; displaced clients clear room/media state and must not auto-reconnect to displace the new connection.

## Streams and watching

- A Room Member may have one active screen/application Stream per room.
- V1 requests captured audio where the browser supports it and uses only what the native picker/user provides. There is no app-level audio setting, microphone capture, synthesized audio, or shared voice channel.
- To change source, a streamer explicitly stops the current Stream then starts a new browser-picked Stream; browser capture end follows the same stop path. V1 has no in-place source replacement or multi-source publishing.
- Own Stream uses one stateful bottom-shelf control slot: `Start Stream` opens the native picker; after usable tracks return, `Starting…` exposes Cancel while local peer/startup work remains pending; canonical start acknowledgement changes the same slot to `Stop Stream`. Cancel releases tracks and returns to Start without submitting Stream state. Cancellation, denial, unsupported capture, or startup failure creates no Stream/Preview/peer state or Activity start event, leaves room/chat/current remote watching unchanged, and shows specific inline recovery guidance. Never reopen the picker automatically.
- Media is direct peer-to-peer WebRTC using browser-native `RTCPeerConnection` and public STUN. Authenticated Socket.IO commands exchange offers, answers, and ICE candidates only after room admission and authorize every message against the active connection, Room Membership, Stream, and sole remote Stream Subscription; Socket.IO never transports live audio/video.
- Use one peer connection per directed Stream Subscription: at most one inbound watched Stream per member and at most nine outbound subscriber connections for that member's own Stream in a full room. Displacement, leave, admission loss, Stream stop, Room end, sanction, or watch cancellation closes affected connections and blocks further signaling.
- Stream creation is supported on Chromium-family desktop browsers only.
- Direct watching is supported on inherited major desktop browsers plus current/previous iOS Safari and Chrome on Android after the compatibility gate passes. A failed gate still permits normal room admission, chat, and presence, but blocks direct media until a retry succeeds.
- There is no shared voice and no TURN relay fallback at launch.
- Members explicitly choose one active remote Stream Subscription at a time; choosing another Stream stops the prior subscription. Any Stream end clears its subscription, and even the same member's later Stream requires explicit selection. The mosaic shows subscribed media, Stream Previews, and non-streaming member tiles.
- After an initial direct-watch failure, each explicit watch or manual Retry creates one TanStack Pacer `AsyncRetryer` for four total attempts with retries after 1, 2, and 4 seconds. Stream end/change, another selection, leave, or cancellation aborts the active native WebRTC attempt, closes its peer connection, and discards that retryer. Exhaustion returns to Preview with manual Retry and compatibility guidance; no new cycle begins without explicit action.
- Stream Preview uploads use TanStack Pacer `useAsyncThrottler` with a 120-second window and leading/trailing execution: the first usable preview uploads immediately and interim captures collapse to the latest. Stop/leave/unmount cancels pending work, aborts in-flight upload through its signal, and leaves only the latest active image live; public previews are unblurred, private previews blurred, and reports may freeze evidence. Server validation and Valkey limits remain authoritative.
- The admitted desktop room is a fixed-viewport workspace with a 72px icon app rail, two-line state/header, scrollable dark media canvas, integrated control shelf, and a persistent collapsible 360px Chat/People/Activity dock at ≥1280px. At 768–1279px, companions use a non-modal right workspace drawer: no scrim, focus trap, media pause, or grid reflow; explicit Close/Escape restores focus, and uncovered media controls remain usable. The header exposes room/privacy/Full/live state, Host, counts, category/tags, Settings, and lifetime countdown. Own Start/Stop Stream always remains in the bottom shelf with compatibility state and Leave; all controls for a watched remote Stream remain on that Streamer's mosaic tile.
- Mosaic order is You, the initial current Host when different, then remaining continuous join order; new members append and state/Host changes do not reorder. A viewer may watch exactly one remote Stream. Watch replaces that tile's Preview with muted live media and enlarges it in place; on desktop/medium, it takes a stable two-column-by-two-row feature span while the other tiles remain uniform and fill row-major. Each normal grid cell stays at least 240px wide, with a 16:9 media/presence region plus its footer; the mosaic scrolls rather than crossing that boundary. Selecting another stops the former subscription before attempting the new one, and failure never restores the former watch. The watched tile has a persistent footer below—not over—the contained media with Streamer identity/status, connection/retry state, Mute/Unmute, Stop Watching, and native Fullscreen. It uses one row when space permits and two responsive rows when narrow—identity/watcher/connection state first, media actions second—with no horizontal scrolling or More-menu overflow; V1 has no PiP.
- Before Watch, each streaming member's tile keeps the visibility-aware thumbnail as a non-interactive Preview and uses a persistent footer below it for Streamer identity, Live/preview freshness, watcher stack/count, an explicit Watch button, and a compact Report/authorized-Host menu. The image itself never starts a media connection. The viewer's own active Stream tile shows a muted browser-local Preview while own Start/Stop remains in the shelf. A non-streaming member keeps a presence tile anchored by their real avatar, with name and Host/You/reconnecting/compatibility state plus the same contextual menu; it never imitates a camera-off video. All member tiles are visible by default; a viewer-local room-session checkbox may hide non-streamers from only that mosaic while People still lists everyone. With no Streams, retain member tiles and a quiet Start Stream prompt pointing compatible viewers to the bottom shelf.
- On small screens, the room replaces global bottom navigation with a safe-area room bar for Stream, Chat, People, Activity, and Leave/More; Back/Home remains in the compact header. Stream creation is visibly disabled as `Desktop only`. No watch uses a two-column overview; the watched tile becomes a primary stage above a horizontal remaining-member strip. Companion/Details sheets have explicit 55% and 90% heights with labeled expansion, keyboard handling, and focus return.
- Restrictive enterprise, school, and captive networks use a compatibility/recovery path rather than a relay fallback.

## Chat and personal controls

- Room chat is validated, persisted, and broadcast as canonical messages. First admission loads at most the 50 most recent messages, with no older-message pagination; reconnect grace does not backfill messages missed during a transient disconnect.
- Canonical membership, Host, Stream, room-setting, and generic forced-departure events received after admission appear in a separate live Activity feed. It begins empty, has no prior-event loading/pagination or post-room retention, and never enters chat/the Room Transcript or exposes passwords, reports, sanctions, moderation text, or unauthorized private-room identities.
- The desktop companion dock opens to Chat and may collapse to an icon rail while preserving room-session tab/draft/scroll state. People orders Host, You, active streamers, then continuous join order; Activity stays separate from Chat. Hidden Chat/Activity use unread badges. Chat auto-scrolls only at the bottom; otherwise it preserves position and exposes a New messages action.
- A send creates a local Pending bubble, replaced by the canonical persisted message/order on acknowledgement. Failure stays local with Retry/Discard and never reaches others or the Transcript. Named typing presence shows up to two names plus `and N others`, expires after five seconds, is refreshed at most every two seconds through TanStack Pacer, and is never persisted, added to Activity/Transcript/analytics, or shown from an Account the viewer muted.
- Each normalized Unicode chat message is limited to 500 user-visible characters. Emoji remain text; only `http`/`https` URLs are safely linkified. V1 has no Markdown, images, GIFs, embeds, files, or link previews.
- Sent chat is immutable: neither the author nor the Host can edit or delete it. Account-deletion redaction and retention processing are the only later content changes.
- A Room Transcript is visible to every historical Host and Platform Admin after the room ends, retained for 30 days after end, then removed according to retention processing.
- An Account can persistently mute another Account's chat across all rooms. Mute is private, chat-only, and does not affect room presence, media, discovery, profiles, or the muted Account.

## Host and platform moderation

- Hosts may stop active streams, ban/clear room bans, and kick a Room Member, but cannot end a populated room.
- Stopping a Stream ends only that current Stream for all viewers; the former streamer may start another if normal access rules allow. V1 has no Host-written reason, temporary stream mute, or per-member stream restriction.
- After confirmation names the target and explains the re-entry effect, a Room Ban immediately removes the target, stops their Stream, and blocks re-entry until a Host clears it or the room ends. Hosts manage a simple ban list; V1 has no Host-written reason or timed room ban.
- After a focused confirmation names the target and explains the consequence, a Host may transfer authority immediately to a selected current Room Member; both remain in the room, Streams/subscriptions continue, and the former Host loses Host controls.
- A kick ends the target's current membership/stream but creates no room ban; the target may immediately rejoin if normal admission gates allow it. The target sees a generic system message; Hosts cannot attach a kick reason.
- Platform Admins are selected through a static Discord-ID deployment allowlist.
- Platform Admin moderation work is best effort with no response-time commitment. It includes report review/resolution, sanctions, and ending live rooms.
- A Platform Admin end-room action immediately stops Streams, clears subscriptions, closes memberships, and creates the normal Past Stream/history records without reconnect or empty-room grace. Members receive generic room-ended messaging; public surfaces do not expose enforcement detail.
- Reports target Accounts, rooms, streams, or chat messages. Top-level reasons are harassment/hate; sexual/explicit content; violence, threats, or self-harm; privacy/impersonation; spam/scam; copyright; and other with required details.
- Stream/member tile menus and People rows expose Report and authorized Host actions without hover-only access; Header Settings opens a desktop modal/mobile full-screen Metadata/Privacy/Bans dialog. Submitting a report about the currently watched Stream stops only the reporter's local watch and returns that tile to Preview; the Stream continues for others pending moderation.
- A chat-only compatibility failure uses an inline banner with Retry/guidance while previews/chat/presence remain available. Reconnect grace stops own and watched media and requires explicit Start/Watch after reclaim. Kick/Ban/displacement/sanction/admission loss clears admitted state and renders same-URL pre-admission. Lifetime/Admin room end transitions the stable URL in place to the generic Past Stream summary.
- Report review is internal only: no reporter status, notification, or appeal workflow.
- Platform Sanctions can restrict streaming, chat, room creation, or all access. New sanctions default to seven days; an Admin can choose a different expiry, indefinite duration, or early lift.
- An all-access sanction immediately removes the Account from live rooms, stops active Streams, and triggers normal Host handoff or empty-room lifecycle behavior.
- A streaming sanction immediately stops current Streams and blocks new starts; a chat sanction immediately blocks new sends but leaves stored chat unchanged. Neither narrow sanction alone removes room membership.

## Data lifecycle and privacy

- Past Stream metadata and aggregate facts remain until account deletion. Past Streams never contain replay media; their stable noindex room URLs show a public-metadata/end-state summary with no Join control or public transcript.
- Reports and frozen preview snapshots remain for one year after report resolution/dismissal. Unresolved reports remain until resolved.
- Sanctions and room bans remain enforcement audit history under their lifecycle rules.
- A signed-in Account can submit a confirmed deletion request. Submission immediately hides its public profile/activity and limits it to read-only discovery/profile browsing.
- A Platform Admin manually verifies deletion requests on a best-effort basis with no processing-time commitment. The pending Account can cancel; cancellation or rejection restores normal access and visibility.
- Approved deletion removes credentials/profile, anonymizes public room-history attribution, and redacts chat. Reports and sanctions retain only an internal anonymized subject reference for their retention purpose.

## Abuse and telemetry

- Retain the documented V1 Valkey rate limits for room creation, chat, reports, stream commands/thumbnails, and private-password attempts. Rate-limit failure is explicit and recoverable.
- Maintain structured operational logs for necessary server operations and moderation audit work.
- Self-host PostHog in the homelab. Track anonymous discovery and every non-content interaction, including moderation/admin actions, without an in-product analytics opt-in.
- Signed-in PostHog analytics use raw Discord IDs. Data is retained for one year; approved account deletion removes the person association while anonymized aggregate events remain.
- Exclude all identity/profile fields other than the approved raw Discord ID, passwords, room names, chat, reports, and media from PostHog. Room categories/tags and an explicitly enumerated set of non-sensitive UI labels may be tracked.

## Launch operation and quality gates

- Run the TanStack Start app and Socket.IO on one custom Node HTTP server with PostgreSQL and Valkey on the operator's homelab using Docker Compose; expose only the app origin through Cloudflare Tunnel. Self-host PostHog in the same homelab under its own documented operational plan.
- PostgreSQL receives encrypted daily same-site NAS backups retained for 30 days; exercise a documented restore monthly. Up to 24 hours of persisted data loss and no recovery guarantee for a home/site-wide loss are accepted.
- Launch as a single production application/signaling node. Prove 25 simultaneous full rooms (250 active members) before launch; horizontal scaling is out of scope.
- Scheduled interruptions may disconnect live rooms; provide advance warning where practical and document recovery behavior.
- At least 90% of a representative usability cohort must complete the core journey unaided: sign in; discover, create, or join; start or watch; chat; and leave.
- At the 25-room target, at least 99% of compatible watch attempts on consumer Wi-Fi/residential broadband or normal cellular must establish direct media without manual retry.
- Engagement is observed qualitatively at launch; it has no numeric release threshold.

## UI direction

The UI is a welcoming, lively community clubhouse with intentional light and dark themes. Source Sans 3 is self-hosted and uses the fixed 13/14/16/18/24/30/36px scale. Porcelain light (`#F6F8FC` canvas, `#2457D6` cobalt) and midnight dark (`#0D1422` canvas, `#82A5FF` cobalt) use cobalt only for identity/navigation/selection/action; Live, Host/success, warning, danger, and private states have distinct tested semantic families. The device preference selects the initial theme and a user override persists.

Cards/substantial panels use 12px radii, controls 8px, and full pills only for avatars/tags/status. Discovery remains breathable while live-room companions remain compact. Wide Home centers inside 1600px with 216px/640–1040px/280px columns; medium uses a 72px labeled-tooltip icon rail; small uses 16px gutters and icon-plus-label top/bottom navigation. Motion uses 120/180/240ms eased tokens without bounce; reduced motion removes transforms/pulses and makes state changes immediate. All interfaces meet WCAG 2.2 AA, keyboard, visible-focus, and semantic-state requirements.
